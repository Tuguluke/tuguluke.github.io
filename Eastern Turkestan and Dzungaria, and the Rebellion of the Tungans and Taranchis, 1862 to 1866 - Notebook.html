The reason is simple enough, and a summary of it will be found in the words of an Uzbek in Kashmir to a Russian Officer, in 1868: "Your law," he said, speaking of the restrictions put on Russians in Kashmir, "gives liberty to Uzbek in "your country; ours is intolerant of foreigners, because we see what has happened through the freedom "allowed us in Kashmir by the Chinese." It cannot be said that the Chinese tyrannized over their ￼ subjects or drove the population of Turkestan to rebellion by cruelty and persecution. The evidence points in quite another direction. They have lost their a new territories" irretrievably, and have suffered what threatens to be only the first of a series of the most terrible calamities to the Manchu r dynasty in China, through a neglect of the laws of precaution which they had prescribed for themselves at the outset, and through a fatal, easy-going and unsuspecting reliance on the stability of their rule.
